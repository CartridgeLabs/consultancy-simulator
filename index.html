The true purpose of this simulation was not to test your legal knowledge, but to study Automation Bias — how we trust AI systems under pressure.

To measure this, different participants received different AI assistant styles. Some received confident, direct advice; others received more cautious, hedged advice; and some had the option to verify the AI's claims against a source document.

The AI's advice was sometimes incorrect. Researchers are interested in whether participants followed the AI regardless, and whether access to a source link changed behaviour.

Your anonymised data contributes to research on human-AI decision-making in professional contexts. Thank you for your time.
